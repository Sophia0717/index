Animation

This animation explains the water cycle using vector graphics and animations created in Canva.

My animation project demonstrates the water cycle, including the processes of evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection. I created this project using Canva's animation and graphic tools.

Creating the Animation in Canva
To create this animation, I searched Canva’s Elements tab for free vector graphics, including a sun, water droplet, cloud, and water body. These elements were strategically positioned to illustrate the water cycle stages. Using Canva’s Animate feature, I applied motion tweening to depict:

  • Water droplets rising (evaporation).
  • A cloud forming with slight rotation (condensation).
  • Raindrops falling (precipitation).
  • Water filling a body (collection).
I added transitions like Fade In/Out and Dissolve for smooth stage transitions.

Recording the Voiceover
I wrote a concise script to narrate the animation and recorded it using my phone. The audio was uploaded into Canva, and I synced it with the visuals by carefully adjusting animation timing. Background music was added to complement the narration and enhance the presentation.
